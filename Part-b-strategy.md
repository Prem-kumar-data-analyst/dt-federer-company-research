# Part B — Sourcing Strategy + Scale-Up Proposal

# Question 1 — Sourcing Methods

## 1. LinkedIn Sales Navigator
Useful for:
- founder background
- hiring activity
- manufacturing roles

Limitation:
- incomplete company data

---

## 2. DSIR Recognition Lists
Useful because:
- identifies R&D-focused manufacturers
- strong signal for technical depth

Limitation:
- not all innovative firms are listed

---

## 3. Industry Exhibitions
Examples:
- CPHI India
- BioAsia
- India Lab Expo

Useful because:
- specialty manufacturers participate directly

Limitation:
- requires filtering service vendors

---

## 4. Export Databases
Useful for:
- identifying export-oriented specialty firms

Limitation:
- revenue data often unavailable

---

## 5. Patent Databases
Useful because:
- patents indicate differentiation

Limitation:
- some SMEs do not patent despite innovation

---

# Question 2 — 1000 Company Proposal

## Week 1
Build raw universe of 5000+ companies using:
- directories
- expos
- LinkedIn
- export lists
- government databases

Expected yield:
5000 raw companies

---

## Week 2
Use AI-assisted filtering for:
- manufacturing validation
- sector classification
- founder analysis
- growth signals

Expected yield:
2000 shortlisted companies

---

## Week 3
Manual qualification:
- ownership checks
- growth validation
- technical differentiation
- Federer scoring

Expected yield:
1200 qualified companies

---

## Week 4
Quality control and enrichment:
- personalization hooks
- duplicate removal
- evidence validation
- outreach readiness

Final yield:
1000 high-confidence ICP-qualified companies

---

# Quality Control

False positives would be reduced through:
- manual spot checks
- ownership verification
- manufacturing evidence validation
- multi-source confirmation

---

# Workflow Philosophy

AI should accelerate research speed, but final judgment must remain human-led.
